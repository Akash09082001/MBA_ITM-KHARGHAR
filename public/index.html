    FAQ 

    Know everything about the MBA program

    • 1. What is the difference between an MBA and a PGDM degree?

      Answer - The primary difference between the two programs is that an MBA is a degree programme, whereas a PGDM is a diploma programme. MBA degree is offered by universities that are private, public, or connected to a university. These universities, which are under the University Grants Commission (UGC), can only award degrees. At the same time, under the Ministry of Human Resource Development (HRD), the PGDM programme is provided by b-schools recognised by AICTE and standalone/autonomous b-schools not under the UGC. Nevertheless, the business sector does not distinguish between the two. Graduates from both schools are qualified for managerial positions of the exact nature.

    • 2. Is prior work experience mandatory before applying for an MBA course?

      Answer - Most B-Schools require graduates to have at least two to three years of experience for MBA course admission. However, many B-Schools in India do not consider previous job experience when determining an applicant's eligibility for MBA admission.

    • 3. Are MBA courses only for entrepreneurs and people interested in beginning a startup?

      Answer - An MBA isn't the ultimate qualification for entrepreneurs. Many successful founders have achieved success without one. However, you will have intense competition and face various challenges when running a successful business. Pursuing an MBA course India is an ideal way to improve your chances to expand your network, strengthen your abilities, and increase the resources at your disposal.

    • 4. Are CAT, GMAT, MAT, or other qualifying examinations mandatory to pursue an MBA?

      Answer - Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) need the CAT, and many other elite management schools also take it. Alternative MBA entry tests, such as XAT, NMAT, SNAP, and CMAT, are recognised by several MBA colleges in India.
